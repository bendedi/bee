What can data say us on bees ?

Humidity

This diagram shows a steady decline in the internal moisture content, almost regardless of the external humidity. Another coded message which means that the bees begin to close (seal) the honey cells with a wax plug. The nectar is indeed transformed into honey only when its internal moisture content is lowered to 14%. Once the operation is complete, the ambient humidity declines. Knowing the hygrometry in a hive is especially important in winter. This is a measure of the risk of bees - which manage cold rather than excess water - exposed to the many diseases caused by humidity. In May, the humidity is the signal of a strong activity and its slow decline confirms that one can envisage a harvest ... as we could verify it in the video below.

Weight

To be able to spend the winter in good conditions a colony must have about 12 kilos of food. Which corresponds to a hive Dadant 10 frames to a weight of 32 to 34 kilos (with the roof and without feeder). Example of weighing by two people with a load cell. Another method with a scale: We go up on the scale while holding the hive and deduce its own weight.
Note :
It is early October that the hive must weigh 32 kg. In September the consumption can reach 4 Kg if the weather conditions are bad.

Temperature

The diagram above shows a progression of the temperature and its maintenance to a level comparable to that of a hive body. This is the sign that a lot of bees are in the hive, where the module has been placed, busy producing honey (outside, the acacia trees are in bloom). Outside the harvest period (roughly between May and July), variations in temperature provide indications of the queen's egg-laying rate. Especially in cold weather, they give valuable information on the ability of the swarm, then gathered in a cluster, to face the frost. In the event of a lasting decrease of the internal temperature below 30 degrees, it will be necessary to bring reserves of food (candied bread, sugared water).
